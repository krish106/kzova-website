Privacy Policy for Arion VPN.

Last updated: May 31, 2026. This page explains how Arion VPN by Kzova Labs handles privacy, advertising, and public VPN infrastructure.

No logs Data we process Ads and consent Public VPN network Contact

1. No-logs policy

Arion VPN does not collect or store browsing history, traffic destinations, DNS queries, VPN session logs, or the content of your internet traffic.

2. Data we process

The app may process basic app state on your device, such as selected server, connection state, premium time balance, reward state, and app settings. This information is used to operate the app experience.

The backend may receive technical route health signals such as failed server identifiers so unhealthy routes can be temporarily penalized. These signals are used for server quality and are not used to track browsing activity.

3. Ads and consent

Arion VPN uses Google AdMob to keep the app free. AdMob may process device identifiers and advertising signals according to Google's policies. In regions that require consent, the app should present privacy and consent options before personalized ads are used.

Rewarded ads are optional and provide Time Bank Boost minutes. The app should not give premium reward if a rewarded ad fails or is not completed.

4. Public VPN infrastructure

Arion VPN may use public volunteer VPN infrastructure such as SoftEther and VPN Gate. These servers are operated by third parties, not Kzova Labs. While Kzova Labs does not log your browsing activity, third-party server operators may have their own systems and policies.

5. Children

Arion VPN is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child provided personal information, contact us so we can review the issue.

6. Changes

We may update this policy when app features, legal requirements, or third-party services change. The updated version will be posted on this page.

7. Contact

For privacy questions, contact Kzova Labs at contact@kzovalabs.com.
